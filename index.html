Hardware for a Gaming Computer!

Storage drive

Determines how much storage you have and how fast files load. I've chosen a Samsung 990 PRO 2TB solid-state drive which has a high speed of 7,450MB/s! A traditional hard-disk drive only reaches up to 220MB/s.

Random access memory

RAM dictates how much tasks can handle at once and how quickly can move between. Kingston Fury Beast 32GB at 6000MHz speed is great for my gaming PC. 16GB and 3200MHz is the usual standard.

Power supply unit

PSU supplies power to all the PC parts and enables stable performance. With a gaming PC with powerful parts, you need a high wattage so you can power everything. Asus Prime 750W Gold is a good choice.

Central processing unit

CPU serves as the brain, determines how fast actions are processed. Intel Core i7-12700F at a high maximum speed of 4.9 Ghz is good for my gaming PC. It has 12 cores, so it can do 12 jobs at once, most CPUs have six.

Graphics processing unit

GPU handles the processing of graphics and visuals. This is very important for a gaming PC. A strong GPU for my gaming PC is an ASRock Radeon RX 9070 Challenger which has 16GB DRAM. 16GB is very high.

Motherboard

Connects all the computer parts together and lets them communicate. Speed isn't a factor, but you want one that supports your parts and has built-in Wi-Fi. An MSI PRO Z790-P meets this criteria.

External hardware
Keyboard

I chose a Logitech G PRO X TKL LIGHTSPEED RGB.

Display

Samsung Odyssey OLED G8 looks nice.

Mouse

Logitech G Pro X Lightspeed 2 has good reviews.
