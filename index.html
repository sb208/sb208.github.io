Hardware for a Gaming Computer!

Storage drive

Determines how much storage you have and how fast files load. I've chosen a Samsung 990 PRO 2TB solid-state drive (SSD) which has a high speed of 7450MB/s! A traditional hard-disk drive (HDD) only reaches up to 220MB/s.

Random-access memory

RAM dictates how many tasks can be handled at once and how quickly can move between them. Kingston Fury Beast 32GB at 6000MHz speed is great for my gaming PC. 16GB and 3200MHz is the usual amount.

Power supply unit

PSU supplies power to all the PC parts and enables stable performance. With a gaming PC's high-powered parts, you need a high wattage so you can power everything. An Asus Prime 750W Gold is a good choice.

Central processing unit

CPU serves as the brain of the PC, determining how fast actions are processed. Intel Core i7-12700F with a high maximum speed of 4.9GHz is good for my gaming PC. It has 12 cores, so it can do 12 jobs at once; most CPUs have only six.

Graphics processing unit

GPU handles the processing of graphics and visuals. This is very important for a gaming PC. A strong GPU for my gaming PC is an ASRock Radeon RX 9070 Challenger which has 16GB DRAM. 16GB is very high.

Motherboard

Connects all the computer parts together and lets them communicate. Speed isn't a factor, but you want one that supports all your parts and has built-in Wi-Fi. An MSI PRO Z790-P meets this criteria.

External hardware
Keyboard

I chose a Logitech G Pro X TKL Lightspeed RGB. It looks cool. It has a 1000Hz polling rate, which means it reports keypresses 1000 times per second. Most keyboards, gaming or non-gaming, have this rate though.
